## Overview

Austria is undergoing significant transformation in its cannabis policy, strongly influenced by neighboring Czech Republic (which legalized in 2024) and Germany (which legalized in 2024). The 2022 reform shifted the focus from criminal to health-based, created a framework for cannabis clubs, and opened space for a robust CBD market.

For tourists, Austria is in a transition moment — more tolerant than it was, but still without a regulated recreational market. Vienna, with its tradition of cultural openness, is the best destination within the country.

> **Practical summary:** Austria is modernizing. Legal and good CBD market. Cannabis clubs emerging. Personal use treated as a health matter, not crime. Vienna is the most cannabis-friendly city.

## The 2022 Reform

Austria's 2022 "Cannabis-Reform" was one of the most significant drug policy changes in the country in decades:

**Reform pillars:**
- **Health approach:** Users referred to counseling instead of court
- **Practical decriminalization:** Personal use possession doesn't result in criminal proceedings
- **Cannabis clubs:** Legal framework created (influenced by Germany)
- **Education:** Prevention and harm reduction programs strengthened

**What didn't change:**
- High-THC cannabis remains illegal for sale
- Trafficking remains a serious crime
- No regulated recreational market

## Influence of Neighbors

Austria is surrounded by countries that have advanced further in legalization:

- **Germany (west):** Legalized 25g possession and clubs in 2024
- **Czech Republic (east):** Legalized 10g possession and clubs in 2024
- **Switzerland (west):** Cannabis pilot program in Zurich and Basel

This geopolitical pressure is one of the main drivers of Austrian reform and discussion about full legalization.

## Vienna — The Scene

Vienna has a rapidly growing cannabis scene:

**Neighborhoods:**
- **Neubau (7th district):** The most alternative and creative neighborhood — highest concentration of CBD stores and most open scene
- **Mariahilf (6th):** Adjacent to Neubau, tolerant and central
- **Prater:** Enormous park — historically informal consumption area
- **Naschmarkt:** Famous market with cosmopolitan atmosphere

**Cannabis clubs:**
- Vienna has seen cannabis clubs emerge after the 2022 reform
- Similar model to German/Czech — private adult associations
- Research specific clubs before visiting — some accept tourists

## CBD in Austria

Austria's CBD market is well developed:

- Stores in all major cities
- Pharmacies with CBD products
- Maximum THC: **0.3%** (EU standard)
- Variety of flowers, oils, and derived products
- Prices: €8–18/g for CBD flower

## Tips for Tourists

**In Vienna:**
- CBD: buy legally — good quality, no risk
- Personal use: prefer private spaces
- Clubs: research before arriving, many have websites

**Outside Vienna:**
- Graz and Salzburg have smaller but present scenes
- Alpine tourist cities (Innsbruck): less tolerance

**Borders:**
- German border: note that Germany allows more (25g) but checks may occur
- Never try to take cannabis out of Austria (or bring it in)

## FAQ

**Will Austria fully legalize?**
The debate is active, especially with pressure from neighbors. The coalition government has divergent positions. No defined timeline, but the trend is progressive.

**Do cannabis clubs accept tourists in Austria?**
Depends on the club. The Austrian legal framework is still consolidating. Some clubs require local residency; others are more open. Research specific clubs in Vienna before arriving.

**Is Vienna better than Berlin for cannabis tourism?**
Berlin still leads — more established, more clubs, more transparent. Vienna is growing rapidly but Berlin has the advantage at this moment.

**Is Austrian police strict?**
For personal use, Austrian police have a more health-based approach since 2022. Large quantities or behavior suggesting trafficking are treated seriously. For discreet personal use: low risk in practice.
