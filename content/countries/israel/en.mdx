## Overview

Israel has one of the most progressive cannabis policies in the Middle East — which, given regional policies, is no small feat. The country broadly decriminalized in 2019, has one of the world's most advanced medical programs, and has been debating full legalization for years.

For tourists, the experience varies greatly by city. Tel Aviv is one of the Middle East's most open cities for cannabis — culturally secular, progressive, with a vibrant scene. Jerusalem is far more conservative. And Israeli airports have some of the world's strictest security — never attempt to pass through them with any substance.

> **Practical summary:** In Tel Aviv, discreet personal use is widely tolerated. Medical cannabis is a world leader. Airports: absolute zero tolerance. Never take cannabis when entering or leaving Israel.

## The 2019 Decriminalization

In 2019, the Israeli government approved regulations that broadly decriminalized personal use:

- **First offense:** Warning
- **Second offense:** ₪1,000 fine (~US$270)
- **Third offense:** ₪2,000 fine
- **Use near minors or in public areas:** Higher fines
- **Above 15g or selling:** Criminal offense — possible imprisonment

The 2019 law is explicit: the goal is **not to criminalize users**, focusing punishments on dealers.

## Medical Program — Global Leadership

Israel is a world reference in medical cannabis:

**Why Israel leads:**
- **Dr. Raphael Mechoulam** — the Israeli scientist who isolated THC in 1964 and CBD in 1963 is considered the "father of cannabis research"
- Decades of research at Israeli universities
- Medical system with dozens of approved varieties and products
- Medical cannabis exports to Europe

**For tourist patients:**
- Cannot bring medical cannabis into Israel without special authorization
- Cannot obtain medical cannabis as a tourist (requires residency)
- CBD (under 0.2% THC) may be found legally in health stores

## Tel Aviv — The Scene

Tel Aviv is the heart of the Israeli cannabis scene:

**Why Tel Aviv:**
- A quintessentially secular city — young, progressive demographics
- Beach, nightlife, and entertainment culture that informally integrates cannabis
- Neighborhoods like **Florentin, Neve Tzedek, and the Port** have concentration of open scene
- Regular informal events and parties
- **Yarkon Park** and the seafront are well-known informal consumption spots

**Tel Aviv vs Jerusalem:**
- Jerusalem is significantly more conservative — greater religious presence, less tolerance
- For cannabis tourism: Tel Aviv is the obvious choice

## Security and Borders

**CRITICAL:** Israel has one of the world's most rigorous airport security systems.

- **Ben Gurion Airport (Tel Aviv):** Extremely rigorous security — multiple rounds of checks, detailed questioning, advanced scanners
- **Never attempt** to enter or leave Israel with any illicit substance — consequences are severe and disproportionate
- **Land crossings** (Jordan, Egypt): equally strict
- Even CBD can cause problems — don't risk it

## Tips for Tourists

**In Tel Aviv:**
- Be discreet, in private or clearly social spaces
- Parks and beaches have informal tolerance — read the context
- Avoid using near police officers or security areas

**In Jerusalem:**
- Far more conservative — avoid public consumption entirely
- Old City area and religious sites: absolute zero tolerance

**Airports and borders:**
- Absolute zero tolerance — don't risk it under any circumstances
- Clean any equipment that might have traces before traveling

## FAQ

**Will Israel legalize?**
The debate is active in the Knesset (parliament). Several bills have been introduced. The political coalition changes frequently — difficult to predict, but the long-term trend is progressive.

**How does Israel compare to other countries?**
Israel's decriminalization (15g, 21+) is relatively generous. And Israel's medical program is decades ahead of most countries. The research ecosystem is world-class.

**Can I buy CBD in Israel?**
CBD products with low THC are available in health stores and selected pharmacies. CBD regulation is less clear — check product THC content.

**What is the relationship between cannabis and Israeli culture?**
Cannabis has deep roots in Israeli counterculture since the 1970s. The legalization movement is strong and well-organized. Israeli scientific research on cannabis has influenced the entire world.
