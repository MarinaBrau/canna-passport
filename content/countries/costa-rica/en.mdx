## Overview

Costa Rica is one of Central America's most popular tourist destinations — famous for ecotourism, beaches, and biodiversity. Regarding cannabis, the country occupies an intermediate position: medical legal since 2020, recreational technically illegal but widely tolerated for discreet personal use.

For tourists, Costa Rica offers a relaxed experience. Police rarely arrest for personal possession, the local culture is laid-back, and beach destinations like Manuel Antonio and Montezuma have established informal scenes.

> **Practical summary:** No dispensaries for tourists, but a tolerant atmosphere. Use discreetly in private spaces. The country's natural beauty is the main attraction — cannabis is a bonus for those who want it.

## The 2020 Law

Law 9795 legalized medical use and scientific research of cannabis in Costa Rica:

- **Who can access:** Patients with a Costa Rican medical prescription
- **Cultivation:** Permitted for medical and scientific purposes under license
- **Import/export:** Regulated by the Ministry of Health
- **Recreational:** Remains illegal by law, but decriminalized in practice

The law did not create dispensaries for recreational use — it was a medical step, not broad legalization.

## Tourist Destinations and Cannabis

### Manuel Antonio
The country's most visited national park. Beaches and hostels nearby have an established informal scene. Very relaxed atmosphere with international tourists.

### Montezuma and Santa Teresa (Nicoya Peninsula)
Surf and hippie-chic destinations with high tolerance. Cannabis circulates among the traveling community. Laid-back environment, ideal for discreet use.

### Puerto Viejo (Caribbean Coast)
Costa Rica's Caribbean side has strong Jamaican Rastafarian influence. Cannabis is part of the local culture — much more open than other regions.

### San José
Capital city with an urban scene. Neighborhoods like **La Californie** and the historic center have a presence, but exercise more caution in urban contexts.

### La Fortuna / Arenal Volcano
Ecotourism destination, more family-oriented. Use exists but is more discreet — a different vibe from beach areas.

## Tips for Tourists

**Safety:**
- Prefer private spaces — guesthouse, Airbnb, reserved area
- Avoid buying from strangers on the street — risk of scam or legal issues
- If approached by police: cooperate, be respectful — confiscation is more likely than arrest

**Local culture:**
- Costa Rica is a proud country, conservative in certain aspects — don't assume universal tolerance
- In more rural and religious regions, the atmosphere differs from tourist beaches
- Costa Ricans (Ticos) are known for hospitality — "pura vida" is real

**Practicalities:**
- Never try to transport cannabis out of the country — airports carry serious risk
- Buying on the informal market is risky — variable quality and legal risk

## FAQ

**Will police arrest for personal possession?**
Rarely. Common practice is to confiscate and release. Arrest can occur with larger quantities or in problematic contexts, but it's the exception for tourists with small amounts.

**Is cannabis easy to find in Costa Rica?**
In popular tourist destinations, yes — through guides, hostels, or local contacts. It's not like Dutch coffeeshops — it's an informal market.

**Can I bring cannabis from another country to Costa Rica?**
Absolutely not. Importing cannabis is a serious crime in any amount. Never cross international borders with cannabis.

**What's the difference between the Pacific and Caribbean coasts for tourists?**
The Caribbean Coast (Puerto Viejo) has Jamaican Rastafarian influence and is considerably more open about cannabis than the Pacific Coast, which is more conventionally touristy.
